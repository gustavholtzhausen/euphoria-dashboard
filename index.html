Macro Context Philadelphia Fed Leading Index (USSLIND) The Philadelphia Fed Leading Index (USSLIND) is a monthly index that forecasts the 6-month growth rate of the coincident index for the United States. It incorporates housing permits, initial unemployment claims, delivery times, and the interest rate spread. The value reported is the 6-month annualised growth rate — positive values indicate expansion momentum, negative values indicate contraction. This indicator is presented here as macroeconomic context alongside the euphoria composite, not as part of the composite score itself. — Not included in composite score
Direction Direction tells you whether the economy is currently expanding or contracting based on the most recent month's LEI reading.

Expanding = the composite of 10 leading indicators moved higher — economic momentum is positive.
Contracting = the composite declined — forward economic conditions are deteriorating.

Note: A single month's direction can be noisy. The 6-month trend is a more reliable signal.

6-Month Growth Rate Month-on-Month (MoM) Change is the percentage change in the LEI index level from the prior month.

Positive = conditions improved vs last month.
Negative = conditions worsened vs last month.

A single negative reading is not alarming. The Conference Board considers the trend significant when MoM is negative for 3+ consecutive months, or when the 6-month annualised rate falls below −4%.

6-Month Avg Rate 6-Month Change is the cumulative percentage change in the LEI over the last 6 months.

This is the Conference Board's primary signal for recession risk:
• Positive = expansion; low near-term recession risk.
• Negative (−1% to −3%) = caution; slowdown likely.
• Negative (below −4% annualised) = elevated recession risk within 6–9 months.

Historically, a 6-month decline below −4% annualised has preceded every US recession.

As of Index Level is the current value of the LEI composite index, benchmarked to 100 at the year 2016.

The absolute level matters less than the direction and rate of change. However, a level that is declining toward or below the benchmark (100) alongside negative momentum is historically associated with late-cycle conditions.

Current level context: above 100 = above 2016 baseline; below 100 = below baseline.
